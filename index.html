What are cyber threats?

Cyber threats are the possible damage or disruption to a computer network or system.

The most common cyber threats are as followed:

Hacking

Hacking is the act of compromising devices and networks through unauthorised access to an account or computer system.

Malware

Malware is software that is designed to disrupt, damage, or gain unauthorised access to a computer system. It is the shortened version of malware.

Ransomware

Ransomware is a type of malware (malicious software) which is specifically designed to block access to a computer system until a sum of money is paid.

Email Compromise

Email comprimise is where people who are not authorised to look at you email have access to it and are able to send and read emails without your acknowledgement

Data Breaches

Data breaches is where you are using another colleague's computer and reading sensitive information without having the right authorisation.

Insider Threat

Insider threat is a threat to an organisation that comes from the individuals who are inside such as ex-employees, current employees or even contractors who gain inside information about the organisations practices e.t.c

Denial of Service

A DOS (Denial Of Service) is a cyber attack in which a perpetrator seeks to make a network unavailable by sending lots of requests to the server which will disrupt their service.

Phishing

This is the fraudulent practise of sending email pretending to be a reputable company. Individuals do this in order to reveal peoples personal sensitive data such as passwords e.t.c

Consequences of Cyber Attacks:

Loss of Data

Loss of data is when you lose critical information, where the information is destroyed by failures of a neglect in storage of transmission. An example is when you don't back up your data and there is a flood. One way to prevent this is to back you your data regularly and store in at an off site place, that is easily accessible instead in case of disaster recovery.

Theft of funds

Theft of funds is when you are an employee or posing as one and you steal critical data that is important to them. An example is when you fire an employee, make sure that their credentials are removed so that they do not have access anymore to the data. One way to reduce it is to have great security such as having encryption, access rights, physical security, and biometrics.

Reputational Damage

Reputational damage is when an individual or organisation makes accusations against your business that in the perspective of others, it looks negative. An example is when you sell a faulty product or you provide a poor service, they will give negative reviews on social media. One way to reduce reputational damage is to plan everything, understand all actions that can affect the public perception, look at stakeholders and understand their expectations.

Adverse Credit Rating

An adverse credit history is a track record of poor repayment history on one or more loans or credit cards. Adverse credit history will be reflected in a consumer's credit report. It will lower their credit score and make it more difficult to get a loan or credit card with the best terms or even to be approved at all.

Breach of Data Privacy

A breach of data privacy is when there is an accidental loss, alteration or unauthorised disclosure of personal data. An example is when 2 people have the same name but the small business gives the wrong email to the other person. A way to reduce a breach of privacy is to allocate every individual a unique id, like a primary key..

Costs of Investigation and Recovery

When you incur a cyber attack, you will always need to pay to investigate how you got attacked and ways mitigate it so that you won't get attacked that way again.

A network is a series of interconnected devices that can transmit, receive and exchange data, voice and video traffic.

(Techterms, 2020)

What are some of the vunerabilities and weaknesses wihtin a network?

A poor network could be a result of a flaw in the hardware or software which is compromised leading to a security breach.

Examples of such vulnerabilities include:

Firewall Monitoring

A firewall is a device that monitors all of the incoming and outgoing traffic. It has a set of rules of which devices to allow on the network and which not to, blocking any device which does not meet the criteria. Without a firewall, any device will be able to connect to the network.

Unauthorised access to a physical device

Gaining physical access to a device is bad because that device may contain sensitive data that should not go out. One way to reduce this is when you walk away from your desk, make sure to lock it.

Unauthorised devices connected to the network

If you have unauthorised devices on your network, that is bad because your network contains a lot of resources that are sensitive and if they get access to the network, then they would have access to all of the data. In order to protect yourself use a firewall.

Downloading a virus

There are many ways to download a virus such as donwloading a suspicious app from the internet or even downloaidng a document from your email, they are hard to spot but once they are downloaded then they will have access to your network or even your device.

Unsecure Wireless Access

Wireless access is when someone externally is controlling your device, they may not be in the same building, country, continent as you. They will be able to control your movements on your computer, allowing them to have full control allows them to do all sorts of activities.

Software updates not maintained

If there are any unmanaged softwares that haven't been updated or patched with the latest version then anyone can exploit the lower versions. If it is easily exploitable then anyone can access information and data that they shouldn't.

Anti-Virus Systems

An Anti-Virus System is a computer program that is used to detect, prevent and protect against malwares sooner.

There are 3 types of packages

  • Free off the shelf packages->Free Off the Shelf Packages are free and available on the internet for download.
  • Purchased off the shelf packages->Purchase off the Shelf Packages are available for download from the internet but a purchase is required.
  • Pre-Installed packages-> Pre installed packages are automatically installed in the operating system.

    • These are some of the features of anti virus systems

    Anti-Theft

    Anti-theft is designed to protected your data and help you recover your device in case of a loss or even theft. This is really helpful, in case you ever misplace your device or your device gets stolen, it is a way of making sure that they do not gain access to your sensitive data.

    Back up software

    Backup software is where it creates a copy of the data that you want stored and saves it in a safe place in the event that your original data is corrupted, infected by malware. It will allow you to download the copies to restore the original.

    Firewall

    A firewall is a device that monitors all of the incoming and outgoing traffic, it sits between your network and your device. It checks any data that is also stored on your device, such as your files, emails e.t.c It has a set of rules of which devices to allow on the network and which not to, blocking any device which does not meet the criteria. Without a firewall, any device will be able to connect to the network.

    WebCam Protection

    Webcam protection is when it prevents malware, application from accessing your PC's webcam without your permission. If you don't turn on webcam protection it may allow malwares to access your webcam and capture images and/ or videos without consent. With Webcam Protection enabled, untrusted applications cannot capture images or videos and send the content outside of your PC to compromise your privacy.

    VPN

    A VPN stands for virtual private network, it essentially provides an encrypted server and spoofs your IP address so it looks like you are in another country. It also disguises your data traffic online and protects it from external access. If your data is Unencrypted, anyone who has access to the network would be able to see all of the data.

    WiFi Scanner

    Wifi Scanner is a tool that will allow you to scan your Wi-Fi network and will help identify and highlight the network's flaws. It looks for any vulnerabilities within the network such as passwords, emails. It also looks for poor encrypted communication.

    Ransomware Rollback

    Ransomware rollback is when you steal data from an individual or organisation and encrypt their system and their files, it also means where you eliminate the ability to recover the data with shadow files that were created by the OS (Operating System).

    Password Manager

    A password manager is a computer program that allows the user to be able to store, generate and manage their passwords for local and online services. It stores all of the passwords in an encrypted database and calculates it on demand.

    Email protection

    Whether the software includes protection for Windows Mail or Outlook

    Anti-pishing

    Provides a browser extension that protects you from phishing attacks.

    Data Privacy

    Data Privacy is handling sensitive data such as financial data and intellectual property and the organisation needs to follow strict rules when collecting, storing and sharing of that information. It is also referred to as information privacy or in the EU, it is referred to as data protection. There are 4 key considerations that are important for protecting your data 1) Encyrption, 2) Security/Permissions, 3) Storage, 4) Retention



    It is important to know the laws that regulate within your business, industry and any outsourced activies.

    Encryption of data

    In order for the data you keep to be kept secure there are many ways to do this. One such way is encryption of data. Encryption of the data - ensuring that the data is kept secure until an authorised person accesses the data. It is important to keep the data encrypted because, you are keeping personal data and so if they were ever to go out then you would be liable.

    Security of data

    Ultimately, you as a company, need to make sure that your customers and employees data is secure from any unauthorised access. This is so that you avoid the cost and time of dealing with issues, such as formal complaints and breaches of personal data. If someone ever gets a hold of your personal information that is very bad because they can do lots of different things such as apply for bank loans, use your credit card, drain your credit card and ruin your credit score etc.

    Storing on the cloud

    If you ever store your data on the cloud then you need to know about storage, retention and security. You need to be aware where your data is stored locally, but you also need to be aware where your data is stored on the server in the cloud, this is because in different countries there are different rules and regulations. Whatever data that you store on the cloud is available for your cloud provider to see, so nothing is private when you upload something to the cloud. If you ever delete anything then your cloud provider will retain that data for at least 3 months in case you need any of the data back.

    Retention of data

    After a user has left your company or an employee then there should be a period of how long they should retain the data for. Usually the time that they should retain the data for is about 6 months. Retaining data longer than necessary takes up unnecessary storage space and costs more than needed. However, retaining important data helps prevent excessive expenditure in the future in case you have to look for a file for legal or organizational reasons. Implement secure access and regularly check the access logs to ensure the right people have access to the right data.

    What to do when you have a cyber incident?

    A cyber incident is an event that could jeopardize the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of digital information or information systems.

    The two things that are very important are information assets and data recovery.

    Information assets

    Information assets is a way to manage your organisations information assets.

    What should be in the register?

  • Hardware- ensure that you have a log of all your devices
  • Software - this should also include your virtual machines
  • User Permissions - including authorised third parties
  • Log of virtual machines

    • You should always make sure that you do updates and patches so that it can removed any bugs or any gaps in the security.

    Disaster recovery--> This is the process of regularly backing up your data and testing procedures

    You should always ensure that you regularly do a disaster recovery drill. This is the process of regularly (once every 6 months) go and recover your data from where it is stored in case you ever need to access it when the time comes.

    There are 3 ways to deal with cyber risks:

    1. Accept it-->This is where you keeo yourself cyber safe and pay for any consequences
    2. Mitigate it--> This is where you pay a retainer to a cyber incidence responce company
    3. Offload it--> This is where you purchase cyber insurance
    This must all be regularly tested.

    However, if you need to remediate a cyber incident. You should look at cyber insurance and cyber specialists.

    Cyber Insurance

    There are 2 types of cyber insurance, there is First-Party insurance and there is also Third Party Insurance. First Party insurance allows you to cover your businesses' own assets. This is where the insurance company will pay you for any direct or indirect costs such as if you lose money, data, intellectual property or even customers to cyber crime. They will even pay for the businesses down time and the reputational damage that the cyber crime has caused. While Third-Party Insurance is where it covers your liabilities, covering the assets of others such as your customers. If a hacker ever got a hold of sensitive data and used that sensitive data to do anything such as use their credit card or withdraw money from their account then the insurance company will cover that. I may also cover any investigations, legal defence or even any civil damages and compensation. IF your business doesn't handle sensitive data such as customers data electronically then having the third-party insurance may not be the most ideal.

    Cyber Specialist

    Cyber specialist play a vital role to a business when it comes to securing your network. They monitor, detect, investigate, analyse and respond to any events that occur. They protect the systems from risks, threats and vunerabilities. They should help update and patch your system
